\begin{abstract}

ETHL is a venue for postgraduate students and early career researchers in Historical Linguistics to consider the role of theory in Historical Linguistics as well as the place of Historical Linguistics within the wider field. We aim to give young researchers a platform to discuss ontological issues of our disciplines and provide the theoretical tools to perform this task. With linguistic departments under threat and funding bodies trending towards impact output, we think it is vital to carve out a space for the discussion of less flashy areas of research and to make opportunities and space for young academics, the future leaders of the field, to shape the future of Historical Linguistics.

\end{abstract}

\section{About ETHL}

ETHL Workshop 2025 is made up of a series of talks at the Linguistics and English Language department (School of Philosophy, Psychology \& Language Sciences, at the University of Edinburgh) and courses oriented to postgraduate students and early career researchers in Historical Linguistics. The workshop spans two days, and includes activities such as Rapid Fire Student Talks (RFST), aimed at showcasing topics researched by PhD students.
